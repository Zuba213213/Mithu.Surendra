The Story
Headline Playfair Display

Born into grief.
Built into love.

Body Copy — Paragraph 1 (Origin) Sourced: transcript arc

My parents fled genocide. Sri Lankan Tamil, carrying a country's worth of grief in their bodies with no language to put it down. They arrived in London and loved the way survivors love — through safety, achievement, and the silence of everything that couldn't be said. I was born into that. I inherited the anxiety before I had words for it.

Body Copy — Paragraph 2 (The Hand) Sourced: transcript + direct quote

I was also born with a hand the world decided was something to stare at. Something to pity. I hid it for years — at cricket, at dinner tables, in meetings, on dates. Every time I hid it, I was agreeing with the world's verdict. Until I stopped.

"It is not actually deformed. It is exactly the way God wanted it to be. It's beautiful."
Body Copy — Paragraph 3 (The Breaking) Sourced: transcript arc

At 24, I hit the floor. Not metaphorically. I was suicidal, alone, and I made a choice — the only choice that actually matters. I chose to live. And then, bit by bit, I pulled myself out.

Pull Quote (mid-column) Playfair italic · Large · Gold border
"Giving up gave me peace. Giving up gave me hope. Giving up helped me connect with God."
Body Copy — Paragraph 4 (Emergence) Sourced: transcript arc

I stood in front of 200 people in Berlin and raised my hand. I told them the truth about it. Something that had been sealed for thirty years cracked open in that room. That moment — I know now — was the first act of the work I was born to do.

I wrote a book in five days in Ubud. I walked into Sri Lanka and felt home in my DNA for the first time. I am a spiritual healer. That's what felt right. That is what this is.

The Method
Method Name Badge (overlaid on photo) Dark card · Gold text

Large display text: The Lion Work

Sub-label: A methodology for turning the wound into power

Intro Copy (right column) Sourced: transcript + name origin

This work has a name.
It was always yours.

The lion was tattooed on his hand — the hand he spent years hiding. The hand that became, when he finally stopped hiding it, his most visible symbol of power. That's what this work does. It takes the thing you've been most ashamed of and walks you toward it, not away from it.

There are four phases. They don't rush. They don't fix. They move through.

Phase Rows — Full Width Below Header 4 phases · Large faded number · Title + description
01

Name the Pain

Radical honesty about where you actually are. Not the version you tell people. The thing underneath that. The wound beneath the wound. You cannot move through what you won't face — and most people are running so fast they haven't stopped to look.

02

Go Into It

Not around it. Not above it. Through it. This is where unconditional love enters — not the fixing kind, not the improving kind. The sitting-with kind. The staying kind. The kind that doesn't flinch.

03

Find the Fuel

The shame becomes the offering. The wound was always the material. This is the alchemy — where what broke you becomes what makes you. It doesn't disappear. It converts.

04

Arrive in Love

A self you don't need to perform. A life that feels like yours. Not a destination you reach and park at — a practice you live. Self-love not as a concept but as something you do, every day, until it becomes who you are.
