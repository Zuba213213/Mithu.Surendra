Who they are

A real person
in real pain.

This person is not broken. They are awake enough to know something is off — and brave enough to be looking for a way through. They've probably tried therapy, read the books, done the journaling. They're not naive. They're just stuck at the level of *thinking* when what they need is to *feel*.

They found Mithu because his rawness cuts through. He doesn't sound like a coach. He sounds like someone who actually walked through it — and they recognize that. They're already 80% sold before they ever reach out. They just need to know there's a path.

The wound they're carrying

What keeps them
stuck.

Before you get on a call, understand this: every client carries a version of one of these wounds. The surface story will be different. The underlying pattern almost never is.

01

The Not Enough Story

Deep-seated belief that they are fundamentally flawed — too broken, too emotional, too damaged to be worthy of love, success, or purpose. Usually installed in childhood. Reinforced by every rejection since.

02

Success Without Feeling

Achieving things — career, relationships, money — but feeling nothing. Going through the motions. Performing life instead of living it. High functioning on the outside, hollow on the inside.

03

The Self-Sabotage Loop

Gets close to something real — a relationship, an opportunity, a moment of joy — and unconsciously destroys it. Does this repeatedly. Knows it's happening. Cannot stop it. This is the wound in action.

04

Disconnected from Body

Lives entirely in the head. Intellectualizes everything. Can describe the problem perfectly but can't feel their way to the solution. The body has been carrying the wound for years — unheard.

05

Shame Isolation

Believes they are the only one who feels this way. The shame of struggling while others seem fine keeps them silent, disconnected, performing. Deeply lonely in a way they can't articulate.

06

Purpose Vacuum

A haunting sense that life should mean more than this. That they are meant for something — but can't find it. The pain isn't always dramatic. Sometimes it's just a persistent, quiet emptiness.

What converts them

What actually
moves them.

This person has seen coaches before. They can smell inauthenticity from a mile away. What converts them isn't a polished pitch — it's recognition. They need to see themselves in what you say.

🔥

Raw Testimony

Mithu sharing his own wound without polishing it. They need to hear: "I've been exactly where you are." Not aspirationally. Specifically.

🤲

Permission to Feel

Someone giving them explicit permission to stop performing, stop fixing, and just feel what's there. This is rare. It's deeply moving when it happens.

👁

Being Truly Seen

When Mithu names something they've never told anyone — and gets it right. That somatic recognition is the moment trust is born. It's felt in the body, not processed in the mind.

🗺

A Clear Path

Not vague transformation promises. A method. Phases. Markers. "This is what we do, in this order, and here's what shifts at each stage." Structure gives them permission to trust.

🏘

Proof of Community

Seeing others going through the same thing without shame. The school is powerful because it normalizes the struggle and breaks isolation — the most paralyzing part of the wound.

⏳

No Rush

Mithu's willingness to hold space without forcing resolution. This person has been pressured to "get over it" their whole life. A coach who waits is revolutionary.

What stops them

Their objections —
and the bridge.

These aren't objections to Mithu specifically. They're the defenses the wound has built up over years. Don't argue with the objection — speak to the wound underneath it.

What they say

"I'm not sure I'm ready yet."

What they mean

They're afraid the pain is too big. Afraid of what they'll find if they actually look. "Whenever you're ready" is the most disarming thing they can hear — because it removes the pressure that's been there their whole life.

What they say

"I've tried coaching before and it didn't work."

What they mean

They got the mindset work, not the body work. They were given tools for the mind when the wound lives in the body. The difference is somatic — and that's where Mithu's method is different.

What they say

"I can't afford it right now."

What they mean

They don't yet believe the investment will work. This is an evidence problem, not a money problem. Testimonials, the free trial, and a clear method are the answer — not a lower price.

What they say

"I don't know if this is for me."

What they mean

They feel shame about needing help. They're not sure they deserve it. The answer is: "This is for people exactly like you — who have been carrying something they were never supposed to carry alone."

Before every call

What to hold
going in.

Use this as a quick reference before any discovery call or coaching session. Know the archetype. Feel their wound. Meet them there first.

Call preparation checklist —

They are not their problem.
They are a person who needs to be seen.

Ask early —
  • Where do you feel this in your body right now?
  • When did this start — what was happening?
  • What have you already tried?
  • What would it feel like to be on the other side of this?
  • What are you most afraid of finding if you actually look?
Remember —
  • Silence is not a problem — let it breathe
  • The surface story is not the wound
  • Resistance means you're close to something real
  • You don't need to fix it today — just hold it
  • They chose to show up — that took more courage than they know
