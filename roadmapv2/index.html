Mithu's entire offer is built on love, abundance, and the freedom that comes from sitting with pain rather than running from it. That message is real — and it's fragile when the person delivering it is operating from financial pressure.

Method Validation

The Lion Work in the field.
What to break.

The five movements exist on paper because the interview pulled them out. Now they need to be run against real people until they either hold or fail. Failure is the data.

01
The Welcome
Does warmth land when someone arrives armoured? What does Mithu do when humour doesn't break the guard? Break signal: Client stays performative 15+ minutes in. The Welcome never completes.
02
The Attunement
Does the felt-sense reading work across different wound types? Does it hold with a highly intellectualised client? Break signal: Mithu names something, client dismisses entirely — no recognition, no shift.
03
The Mirror
What happens when naming the thing triggers collapse rather than recognition? How does Mithu hold it without retreating? Break signal: Naming the wound re-traumatises rather than opens. Client shuts down completely.
04
The Alchemy
Does the wound-to-resource conversion happen naturally or feel forced? What client types resist the reframe? Break signal: Client hears "your wound is your power" as toxic positivity, not liberation.
05
The Expansion
Does the send-off produce real forward movement or just a good feeling that fades by next week? Break signal: Client feels great in session, unchanged 48 hours later. Expansion doesn't hold.

The goal is not to protect the method. The goal is to find where it fails.

After each session: a 5-minute voice note. What movement stalled? What surprised you? What question opened something unexpected? What did you do instinctively that you wouldn't have planned? The patterns across 3 people and 8–12 weeks are what turn a documented framework into something teachable, sellable, and defensible.

If a movement consistently breaks, that's not failure — that's discovery. It either gets rebuilt or the readiness criteria tightens so the method only runs with the clients it's actually built for.
