01

Origin Story Narrative Arc

The arc follows the classic hero structure but arrives through a distinctly Tamil-London-spiritual path. It moves in three clean phases: the accumulated wound, the breaking point that became a portal, and the work that emerged from the other side.

W1

The Inherited Wound

Born into Generational Grief

Sri Lankan Tamil parents — genocide refugees — arrive in London carrying unspeakable pain with no language to process it. They love the best way they know: through education, status, achievement. Mithu enters the world already inheriting a lineage of anxiety, shame, and survival mode.

The deformed hand arrives not as a problem, but as the thing onto which all existing shame gets projected. A gray cloud. He hides it for years — in cricket, at dinner tables, at networking events.

W2

The Private School Exile

Inferior in Both Worlds

Moved to a private school on a bursary — too poor to belong there, too educated to belong back. Inferiority becomes the operating system. Forced studying until 1am. No one teaching him to do laundry, get haircuts, take care of himself. His wellbeing was not part of the curriculum.

W3

The Substitute Family

Belonging Found, Then Lost

The Noah Hill friend group becomes everything — "more than friends, they were family." The first real belonging. Then systematically dismantled by betrayal. Kavan with Hannah. The Tuna-Ajit situation. Each time he walks away intact, morally, but with another wound in his chest where belonging used to be.

★

The Breaking Point — January 2020

Suicidality as a Portal

Rock bottom. Suicidal. Alone in it. Teal Swan releases a video titled How to Overcome the Desire to Kill Yourself at the exact moment he needs it. This is his first encounter with the idea that the universe responds — that someone or something is paying attention to his pain.

He does not die. He decides to live. And from that decision, the real work begins.

"Only when you want to kill yourself can you decide that you want to live."

A1

First Awakening

Berlin, 2018 — The Hand in Public

At Julian Blanc's workshop in Berlin, in front of 200 people and cameras, he raises his hand and says: I am insecure about my hand. He describes it like King Arthur pulling the sword from the stone. Slow, painful, and once done — a massive, irreversible release.

This is the first time he chooses visibility over shame. It becomes the foundational act of everything that follows.

A2

The Dark Night — Tulum, 2024

Everything Gone, Everything Found

He is broke, alone, injured (quad bike crash), in daily anxiety spirals, unable to work. Naked on a concrete floor in a puddle of his own tears, snot, and saliva. In this moment, he decides to love himself. Not as a concept. As a declaration.

Two mushroom journeys process what years of coaching and therapy couldn't. He connects with God as a direct, living relationship. The dark night ends not with rescue but with surrender — and surrender, he finds, gives peace.

A3

The Emergence

Bali — Sri Lanka — The Book — The Work

A synchronicity (the 33/NDL car plate) leads him to Bali by way of Sri Lanka — ancestral home — where he feels, for the first time, a sense of belonging encoded in his DNA. He writes a channeled book in five days (What's Your Biggest Pain?). He becomes a spiritual healer. He walks slowly through Ubud, present, gorgeous, radiating.

The wound did not disappear. It became the offer.

02

The Intuitive Methodology — Named Phases

Mithu does not run a fixed protocol. He runs an attunement process — but across the transcript, clear phases emerge with observable markers at each transition. These are the bones of his methodology.

Phase One

The Welcome

Warmth Before Depth

He enters through humour, presence, and genuine delight in the other person. No agenda. This is not a technique — it is his natural warmth. The purpose is nervous system safety. The client needs to feel that they are not being assessed or fixed. They are being met.

Observable Transition Markers

Laughter appears — the client's guard begins to lower
The client starts talking about themselves without being asked directly
Mithu becomes quieter — he moves from initiating to receiving

Phase Two

The Attunement

Reading What Is Actually Present

He reads the room at a felt-sense level — what is the person hiding, what is the real pain beneath the presenting issue, where is the energy contracted. This is the same skill he used on the street (give value to what's actually in front of you, not what you want to sell) and in his sessions with trauma survivors. He does not map the client onto a model. He reads them.

Observable Transition Markers

He names something the client hasn't said out loud yet
The client's eyes change — recognition without words
A pause enters the conversation that wasn't there before

Phase Three

The Mirror

Naming the Hidden Thing With Love

This is the core move. He names what is true — the wound, the pattern, the thing they are hiding — not as a diagnosis but as a loving recognition. His Berlin moment is the model: slow, public, painful, and releasing. He holds space for the person to do their Berlin moment. The mirror only works because it is unconditional. He is not trying to fix them. He is seeing them.

"Now it's like: sit with unconditional love. Whereas back then it was: let's try and fix this."

Observable Transition Markers

The client says "no one has ever said that to me before" or similar
Emotion arrives — often suddenly, as if a valve opened
The client stops performing and starts being

Phase Four

The Alchemy

From Wound to Resource

He does not leave the client in the wound. He walks with them through the conversion — the same alchemy he has performed on himself repeatedly. The hand becomes the lion tattoo. The shame becomes the offer. He does this through questions, through story, through direct transmission of his own lived experience of making the same turn. This is where the work becomes theirs, not just his.

Observable Transition Markers

The client starts reframing their own story — spontaneously, without prompting
Energy in the room (or call) shifts — from heavy to something lighter
The client begins asking different questions: "what if" rather than "why"

Phase Five

The Expansion

Leaving Bigger Than They Arrived

The end of a Mithu session is not a summary. It is a send-off. He sends people back into their lives with more of themselves than they had when they walked in. His marker of success is not whether they feel good — it is whether they feel more real. More capable of being seen. More willing to take up space.

Observable Transition Markers

The client says something they couldn't have said at the start
There is a specific decision, intention, or action they name before leaving
Something about the way they carry themselves changes — posture, breath, pace of speech

The Transformation He Delivers

This is the before-and-after state his clients move through. Mapped from what Mithu describes about his own journey and what he names as the work he does with others.

Hiding the thing they're most ashamed of
Living to be seen by others; dying when they're not
Performing love, connection, and belonging
Running from pain at a billion miles an hour
Trying to fix, control, and escape emotion
Sense of worth tied to external achievement
Waiting for someone to finally witness them

→

Note on register: Mithu code-switches naturally. With close friends and in his own head, he speaks raw — profanity, abbreviation, South London cadence. In his work context, he speaks with precision and care but never loses the directness or the warmth. Copy should not sanitise either mode. The rawness is part of the authority.

Copy Rules Derived from Voice

Never Passive He uses active, declarative language. "I decided." "I chose." "I went." "I walked away." Copy should follow this — active verbs, direct address, no hedging.
Pain First He always opens with the real, including the dark parts. Copy that leads with aspiration without acknowledging the pain will feel hollow to his clients. Lead with the truth of the wound before the offer of the transformation.
God Is Present He speaks about God as a direct relationship, not a concept. This is not religious language — it is experiential. Copy should not scrub this out to be more "universal." It is specific and that specificity is the trust signal.
No Faking "You can't lie. You can't fake this shit." Any brand language that sounds like it's performing wellness, masculinity, or spirituality will read as exactly what he rejects. The voice must carry the weight of having actually been through it.
Warmth Before Wisdom He is funny before he is profound. Copy should not open with a teaching — it should open with presence, warmth, something human. The depth lands harder when it's arrived at, not announced.
Specificity Wins The red lights on the London Eye. The number 33. The concrete floor. The lion tattoo. Specific images carry more emotional weight than general statements. Copy should use the real, particular details of his life — not abstracted summaries.
