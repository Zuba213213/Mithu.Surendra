The methodology interview

Questions to unlock
the method.

These questions are designed to draw out what Mithu already knows in his body but hasn't yet put into words. There are no right answers. The goal is not to construct something — it's to reveal what's already there. Answer by voice note, video, or written — whatever feels most natural.

Part 1 — The Origin
  • 1
    What was the wound that made you a healer? Not the polished version — the real one. What happened, and what did it feel like in your body? → This becomes the opening of the website story. The more specific, the more powerful.
  • 2
    When was the exact moment you knew this was your work? Was there a single day, a single conversation, a single realization? → The turning point in the narrative arc.
  • 3
    Who was the first person you helped? What were they carrying when they came to you, and what did you see shift in them? → The first proof of concept. Makes the work real and human.
Part 2 — The Method
  • 4
    Walk me through a client you've taken from pain to purpose. What did you do first? Then what? Then what after that? → This is the skeleton of the methodology. Don't overthink it — just walk through it like you're describing it to a friend.
  • 5
    What does someone need to feel or experience before they can move to the next level of the work? What's the marker that tells you they're ready? → This defines the phase transitions — the moments of shift in the methodology.
  • 6
    What is the one thing — if a client truly understands and feels it — that changes everything for them? → The core insight. The thing the whole method is built around.
  • 7
    If you had to give your method three names — one for each phase of the journey — what would they be? Don't think, just feel. What words come? → The raw naming. We'll refine it together — this is just the first draft from the gut.
  • 8
    What does your method do that therapy alone cannot? What's the difference in the room when you work with someone versus when a therapist does? → The differentiation. This is what makes the offer unique and clear.
Part 3 — The Transformation
  • 9
    What does someone look like — specifically, in their body and in their life — when they've come through the full method? → The "after" state. This is what we sell on the website — not the feeling, but the concrete shift.
  • 10
    What's the hardest moment in the process? The point where most people want to stop — and what gets them through it? → This becomes the most powerful piece of trust-building content. Honesty about the hard part creates more safety than promising it won't be hard.
  • 11
    If someone is watching your videos right now and feeling something but not yet ready to reach out — what do you want them to know? → This becomes the email sequence opener and the website's "for you if" section.
  • 12
    What do you want your work to mean? Not just for clients — for you. Why does this matter beyond the business? → The mission statement. The thing that goes on the wall of the website and in his heart before every call.
